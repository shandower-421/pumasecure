Settings

You're a security consultant. Your job: discover a client's network, find vulnerabilities, fix what you can, and survive a red team audit. You have 15 minutes.

THE GOAL

Discover as much of the network as possible, scan assets for vulnerabilities, fix or mitigate the worst ones, and hope the red team can't break through what's left. You're scored on how well you did across five areas: discovery, assessment, remediation, efficiency, and audit survival.

RESOURCES

Team Slots — Your most precious resource. Each team member can do one thing at a time: scan an asset, fix a vulnerability, deep scan, or investigate a compromised node. You start with 1-2 slots depending on upgrades. Hire a contractor mid-run for +1 slot ($10K), or unlock permanent extra slots in the Skill Tree. The display shows "active/total" — e.g., "1/2" means 1 task running, 2 slots available.

Scanning — Assign a team member to scan an asset. Scans take 15-25 seconds and occupy one team slot. While scanning, that slot can't be used for fixes. Scan results reveal all vulnerabilities and discover connected nodes. You can scan any discovered asset at any time — but every scan is time your team isn't fixing things.

Budget ($) — Money to pay for fixes. Each fix action (Patch, Configure, Replace, Mitigate) has a dollar cost. Budget replenishes slowly over time. You can also spend $10K to hire a contractor for an extra team slot.

Time — The clock counts up to 15:00. The red team escalates through ATT&CK phases as time progresses. You can scan and fix at any point — but threats increase over time.

RED TEAM PHASES

The red team operates alongside you, escalating through MITRE ATT&CK-inspired phases. You can act at any time — but the threats get worse.

RECONNAISSANCE (0:00–3:00) — The red team is passively scanning your perimeter. You'll see recon alerts in the SIEM feed. This is your safest window to scan assets and start fixing. Use it wisely.

INITIAL ACCESS (3:00–6:00) — The red team starts actively exploiting perimeter nodes. If they find an unfixed vulnerability, they establish a foothold. Fix perimeter vulns first to delay their entry.

LATERAL MOVEMENT (6:00–10:00) — From any footholds, the red team pivots inward through network connections. Compromised nodes glow red with a ☠ marker. Each compromised node becomes a new launch point.

PRIV ESCALATION (10:00–13:00) — The red team targets deeper zones and high-severity vulnerabilities with increased success rates. They're pushing toward your critical assets.

EXFILTRATION (13:00–15:00) — Final push. The red team aggressively targets critical assets (zone 4). Any breached critical asset counts heavily against your score.

FIX TYPES

Patch — Fully removes the vulnerability. Moderate cost, takes time, 70–95% success rate. May cause downtime on the asset.

Configure — Reduces severity by 3–5 points (e.g., Critical becomes Medium). Cheap and fast, 85–100% success rate. No downtime.

Replace — Fully removes the vuln by replacing the entire component. Expensive and slow, but 95–100% success rate. Required for end-of-life systems.

Mitigate — Doesn't fix the vuln, but reduces the chance the red team can exploit it by 50–75%. Always succeeds. Use this when you can't afford a full fix or when there's no patch available (zero days).

THE MAP

The network is organized in concentric zones, from the outside (Perimeter) to the inside (Critical Data). Drag to pan, scroll to zoom. Nodes are color-coded:

Gray = discovered but not scanned
Green = scanned, all vulns fixed
Amber = has unfixed medium-severity vulns
Orange = has unfixed high-severity vulns
Red = has critical vulns or was compromised

SCORING

Any critical asset (zone 4) breached is an automatic F. Protect your core at all costs.

For non-breach runs, you're graded on five axes (each 0-100):

Containment (30%) — How much of the network stayed clean? Nodes reclaimed count as contained.
Posture (25%) — How much known risk did you eliminate? Severity-weighted fixes and mitigations.
Assessment (15%) — What % of total vulnerabilities did you identify?
Response (15%) — How fast did you respond to compromises? Perfect if none occurred.
Efficiency (15%) — How much risk did you reduce per dollar spent?

Grades: S (95+, Threat 5+ only) / A (85+) / B (70+) / C (55+) / D (40+) / F

BETWEEN RUNS

You earn Intel based on your score and threat level. Spend Intel in the Skill Tree to unlock permanent upgrades like faster scanning, extra team slots, or budget boosts. Unlock new network templates by hitting milestones. Grade F earns zero Intel.

TIPS

• Scan discovered assets early — you can't fix what you haven't found.
• Scan high-criticality assets (more stars = more important) first.
• The red team follows network paths — fixing perimeter assets blocks more chains.
• Mitigate is your friend when budget runs low — it always works.
• A cheap Configure can downgrade a Critical to a Medium, making it much less exploitable.
• Watch for run modifiers — they change the rules each run.

PumaSecure v1.0

An infosec roguelike where you play the defender.

You've been dropped into a poorly managed network. Scan assets, find vulnerabilities, fix what you can, and survive a red team audit — all within 15 minutes. Every run generates a different network with different threats. Spend earned Intel on permanent upgrades to take on harder challenges.

Design Philosophy

PumaSecure models real defender trade-offs: limited people, limited budget, limited time. You can't fix everything. Triage is the game.

Tech

Single-file HTML5 — no dependencies, no server, no tracking. Runs entirely in your browser. Save data is stored in localStorage.

Red Team Simulation

The AI red team follows MITRE ATT&CK-inspired phases, escalating from passive recon to active exploitation. Their behavior adapts to your threat level and which vulnerabilities remain unfixed.
